# SabaiPICS Branding Brief

> **Name Change:** Formerly "FaceLink", now **SabaiPICS** (สบายพิกส์)
> - "Sabai" (สบาย) = Easy, relaxed, comfortable
> - "PICS" = Photos

**Status:** Starting point for design/branding work

**Date:** 2025-12-02

---

## Brand Positioning

**For:** Thai event photographers who care about reliability, cost, and simplicity.

**Not for:** Casual users, DIY hobbyists, or feature collectors.

**Core belief:** Photographers' livelihoods depend on photo delivery working. Quality infrastructure matters more than flashy features.

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Personality
- **Approachable, not salesy** - Speak like a colleague, not a marketing robot
- **Photographer-focused** - Understand their world (events, deadlines, client expectations)
- **Straightforward** - No jargon, no hype, no unnecessary complexity
- **Confident but humble** - We know our tech is solid. We don't need to shout about it.

### What we DON'T sound like
- ❌ Tech-heavy jargon ("leveraging cloud-native AI paradigms")
- ❌ Hype marketing ("revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing")
- ❌ Cheesy ("smile, you're on camera!")
- ❌ Condescending ("even you can use this!")

### What we DO sound like
- ✅ "Your photos deserve reliable infrastructure"
- ✅ "Save time, not corners"
- ✅ "Infrastructure that works so you don't have to think about it"
- ✅ "Photography deserves better"
- ✅ "Photo delivery, sabai sabai" (relaxed, easy)

---

## Key Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Reliable
**Idea:** Your photos are your business. They deserve enterprise-grade protection.

**Designer direction:** Communicate trust, stability, durability. Show this through design language (solid, grounded, secure).

### Pillar 2: Accurate
**Core idea:** AI search that actually works.

**Designer direction:** Communicate precision, clarity, efficiency. Emphasize speed and reliability.

### Pillar 3: Transparent
**Core idea:** Pricing and features you understand immediately.

**Designer direction:** Communicate simplicity and honesty. Use clear visual hierarchy, avoid confusion.

### Pillar 4: Affordable
**Core idea:** Professional quality at photographer-friendly cost.

**Designer direction:** Communicate value. Show the relationship between cost and quality.

---

## Landing Page Structure

**Key sections to communicate:**
- Hero headline: Lead with primary value (reliability or affordability)
- Subheadline: Support the hero with 2-3 key differentiators
- Pricing cards: Show 3 tiers (STARTER 150฿, PROFESSIONAL 450฿, STUDIO 1,000฿)
- How it works: Photographer + participant flows (visual)
- CTA: Clear primary action

**Designer notes:**
- Let the design communicate value, not just words
- Use photography prominently (showcase real event photos)
- Pricing section should be crystal clear (no hidden details)
- Flows should be visual/minimal text

- Shoots 4-10 events per month (weddings, corporate, parties)

## Visual Identity Direction

### Style Reference
**Modern SaaS:** Vercel, Runway, Linear

**Characteristics:**
- Minimal, clean design
- Heavy use of white space
- Black + white + ONE accent color
- Typography-forward (less decoration, more type)
- Subtle animations (not heavy)
- Photography-friendly (great showcase for photos)

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors:**
- Black: #000000 (main text, dark elements)
- White: #FFFFFF (background, light elements)
- Gray: #F3F4F6 (subtle backgrounds, dividers)

**Accent Color:** #9B8FC7 (Muted Purple)
- Used sparingly (buttons, links, highlights)
- Should feel modern, not corporate
- Should work on both black and white

**Examples of accent color usage:**
- Primary CTA button
- Links and hover states
- Accent bars, badges
- Highlight key stats
- Underlines, accents on headers

### Typography

**Primary Font:** Modern sans-serif
- Examples: Inter, Sora, Space Grotesk, Outfit
- Should be clean, readable, not trendy

**Usage:**
- Headings: Bold, generous sizing
- Body: Regular weight, good line height (1.6+)
- Code/pricing: Monospace (display cost clearly)

### Imagery

**Photo Treatment:**
- Large, showcase photographer work
- No stock photos (authentic)
- Show real event photos (weddings, corporate events)
- Black and white + accent color for design elements

**Icons & Spot Illustrations:**
- Minimal, line-based icons (not filled/heavy)
- Consistent stroke weight across all icons
- Accent color (#9B8FC7) for highlights/accents
- Simple, geometric, clean aesthetic (like Notion)
- Used for: feature callouts, empty states, process steps, success states
- NOT used for: heavy decoration, patterns, background fills

**Illustration Style (Notion-inspired):**
- Flat illustrations (no 3D depth, no shadows)
- Geometric and simple shapes
- Muted color palette (can use accent color as one element)
- Minimal line weight
- Purpose-driven (communicates a concept, not decoration)
- Examples of usage: "How it works" section, empty upload state, successful search result
- Keep illustration count minimal (less is more)

## Design System Basics

### Spacing
- Use consistent 8px grid (8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64, 80px)
- Generous whitespace (not cramped)

### Buttons
- Primary: Accent color background, white text
- Secondary: Black border, black text, white background
- Hover: Subtle shadow or opacity change (no dramatic effects)

### Cards/Sections
- Subtle border (light gray) or shadow (very light)
- 8-16px padding, clean
- Not heavy or "boxed"

### Overall Vibe
- Professional but friendly
- Modern but not trendy
- Clean but not sterile
- Technical but not intimidating

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO
- ✅ Use white space generously
- ✅ Let photography shine
- ✅ Use accent color sparingly (one or two key places per page)
- ✅ Keep copy short and direct
- ✅ Show real event photos
- ✅ Emphasize reliability and transparency
- ✅ Use photographer language ("events," "participants," "delivery")

### DON'T
- ❌ Use stock photography
- ❌ Make accent color too bright or use too much
- ❌ Add unnecessary decoration or illustrations
- ❌ Use tech jargon in copy
- ❌ Over-explain features (show, don't tell)
- ❌ Use animated gifs or video backgrounds (too heavy)
- ❌ Crowded layouts (embrace whitespace)
